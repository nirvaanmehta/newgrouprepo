# Incidence and Prevalence Ratios

## Prevalence
Prevalence is the proportion of a population that has a disease or condition at a specific time.

### Formula
prevalence = existing cases / total population

## Incidence proportion
Incidence proportion is the proportion of initially disease-free individuals who develop the condition during a defined time period.

### Formula
incidence proportion = new cases / population at risk at start

## Prevalence ratio
The prevalence ratio compares prevalence between two groups.

### Formula
prevalence ratio = prevalence in exposed group / prevalence in reference group

## Risk ratio
A simple incidence comparison between groups is often framed as a risk ratio.

### Formula
risk ratio = cumulative incidence in exposed group / cumulative incidence in reference group

## Interpretation
A ratio above 1 suggests higher prevalence or risk in the exposed group, while a ratio below 1 suggests lower prevalence or risk.
