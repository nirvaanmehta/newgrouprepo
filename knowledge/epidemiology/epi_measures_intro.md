# Introduction to Epidemiologic Measures

## Why measures matter
Epidemiology often focuses on how often diseases or health outcomes occur and how those frequencies differ across groups.

## Counts, proportions, and rates
- A count is the number of observed events or cases.
- A proportion compares cases to a relevant denominator.
- A rate often includes a time component or person-time denominator.

## Why denominators matter
A larger number of cases does not always mean higher disease burden if the population size differs.

## Simple starter concepts
For beginners, prevalence and incidence proportion are useful first measures because they help distinguish existing disease burden from new disease occurrence.
